## 1. Constitutional posture

Canon remains a distinct promotion-only boundary.

It does not arise automatically from:

- recurrence,
- runtime stability,
- replay success,
- query usefulness,
- substrate organization,
- long retention,
- packet existence,
- dossier existence,
- readiness posture,
- or review convenience.

Runtime is not canon.
Query is not truth.
Substrate is not ontology.
Consensus is promotion-only. 

Therefore, canon activation must be:

- explicit,
- review-bound,
- claim-scoped,
- evidence-backed,
- contestable,
- and governable.

Canon promotion is a trust uplift, not a storage optimization and not an interpretive mood.

---

## 2. Why this note exists

Door One is already accepted enough for the current phase without active canon minting, and Door Two is supposed to begin only when promotion criteria and temporal trust questions become the real bottleneck. 

That bottleneck is now active.

Door One can already produce:

- lawful runtime memory artifacts,
- replay-honest lineage,
- cross-run evidence,
- bounded recognition,
- real-source comparison,
- structural transition/support probes,
- distortion audits,
- pinned packets and archive bundles. 

What Door One still must **not** do is silently promote any of that into trusted canon.

This note exists to make the promotion boundary explicit enough that:

- Door Two can begin lawfully,
- Door One uncertainty is exposed rather than bulldozed,
- and early canon remains narrow, scoped, reversible, and auditable.

## 3. Core activation rule

**Canon should promote bounded claims, not vibes.**

A candidate may be promoted only when all three top-level gates are satisfied:

1. **Legitimacy**  
   The claim is trustworthy enough to uplift at all.

2. **Utility**  
   The promotion materially improves lawful reuse under declared conditions.

3. **Governability**  
   The promoted claim can remain bounded, contestable, and non-distorting as a standing reference surface.

If any gate fails, the candidate must not promote.

If a candidate is promising but overbroad, the correct action is usually:

- narrow the claim,
- lower the scope,
- request more evidence,
- or keep the packet in review posture.

---

## 4. Promoted primitive rule

The primary promoted unit is expected to be:

**a bounded claim**

not:

- an object in the abstract,
- a basin as ontology,
- a runtime summary,
- a convenience digest,
- a support descriptor by itself,
- or a vague “important structure.”

Objects, cohorts, packets, and runtime artifacts may support a canon claim.

They are not automatically the promoted primitive.

Example preferred style:

> Under declared lens L and cohort scope S, reference pattern R is trusted as a stable baseline comparison anchor for use U.

This keeps early canon:

- claim-level,
- narrow,
- lens-scoped where needed,
- and auditable.

## 5. Gate 1 — Legitimacy

### 5.1 Core question

**Can this bounded claim be trusted enough to uplift at all?**

Legitimacy is about evidence sufficiency and trustworthiness, not usefulness.

### 5.2 Minimum legitimacy requirements

A candidate must satisfy all of the following:

- **bounded claim present**  
  The proposed claim is explicit and narrow.

- **declared lens present**  
  The observational lens is fully declared.

- **support basis present**  
  The exact evidence surfaces are named.

- **replay-honest lineage present**  
  Receipts or equivalent durable lineage support the claim.

- **scope honesty present**  
  Family, cohort, run, temporal, and reuse scope are explicit.

- **non-claims present**  
  The packet states what is not being claimed.

- **ambiguity posture declared**  
  Remaining ambiguity is visible rather than erased.

### 5.3 Strong legitimacy indicators

A candidate becomes stronger when it also has:

- cross-run support,
- real-source support or mixed-source support where appropriate,
- lens variation tested,
- low or bounded distortion severity,
- preserved distinctions clearly named,
- failure risk if wrong explicitly bounded.

### 5.4 Legitimacy blockers

A candidate must fail legitimacy if any of the following are true:

- the claim is not explicit,
- the lens is underdeclared,
- support basis is too weak or missing,
- ambiguity is authority-breaking,
- scope is overbroad relative to evidence,
- explicit non-claims are absent or entirely missing,
- runtime stability is being mistaken for trust,
- substrate organization is being promoted as ontology,
- packet existence is being used as surrogate legitimacy,
- summary surfaces outrank underlying evidence.

### 5.5 Legitimacy outputs

Recommended review outcomes at this gate:

- `legitimate_enough_for_review`
- `request_more_evidence`
- `request_narrower_claim`
- `request_lower_scope`
- `reject_for_legitimacy`

## 6. Gate 2 — Utility

### 6.1 Core question

**If legitimate, is this promotion worth doing?**

Utility is about lawful reuse value, not about truth.

### 6.2 Utility rule

A candidate should promote only if it materially improves one or more of:

- repeated comparison efficiency,
- stable reference anchoring,
- bounded anomaly baselining,
- trusted recall for an authorized narrow use,
- packet burden reduction,
- lawful reuse compression under declared scope.

### 6.3 Reuse compression principle

A core utility measure is:

**reuse compression benefit**

Meaning:

how much repeated lawful re-establishment effort is saved by promoting this claim instead of re-opening the full packet every time.

This may include:

- fewer repeated packet inspections,
- less repeated baseline reconstruction,
- simpler comparison against a trusted anchor,
- lower review burden for the same bounded use.

### 6.4 Utility questions

Ask:

- Without canon, what must be repeatedly recomputed or reinspected?
- With canon, what can now be skipped safely?
- Is the benefit local and narrow, or temptingly broad?
- Does this candidate reduce lawful effort or merely add prestige?
- Does the claimed benefit actually belong to canon, or would a better report/pin packet already solve it?

### 6.5 Utility blockers

A candidate should fail utility if:

- promotion adds little beyond the existing packet,
- the claimed benefit is only rhetorical,
- the benefit comes mainly from unjustified scope inflation,
- a preservation or report surface would solve the problem without trust uplift,
- the reuse compression benefit is trivial,
- the allowed use is unclear.

### 6.6 Utility outputs

Recommended review outcomes at this gate:

- `utility_sufficient`
- `utility_low_keep_packet_only`
- `utility_needs_narrower_use`
- `utility_better_served_by_report_surface`
- `reject_for_utility`

## 7. Gate 3 — Governability

### 7.1 Core question

**If promoted, can this claim remain safely bounded, contestable, and non-distorting as a standing reference surface?**

Governability is about post-promotion behavior.

### 7.2 Governability rule

A candidate should promote only if its authority can be contained safely in practice, not just in principle.

This means the candidate must be governable with respect to:

- scope inflation,
- authority gravity,
- contestability,
- revocation/supersession,
- lens-scope visibility,
- blast radius if misused,
- and canon-ineligible attachments.

### 7.3 Governability questions

    Ask:

        - Will later users overread this as more universal than it is?
        - Will its lens scope be forgotten in practice?
        - Will it distort later interpretation by becoming a shortcut?
        - Is there a clean path to challenge it?
        - Can it be narrowed, superseded, or revoked?
        - If it is wrong, what later work becomes distorted?
        - Are any attached support fields likely to be mistaken for canon themselves?

### 7.4 Governability blockers

    A candidate must fail governability if:

        - it invites obvious scope inflation,
        - it is too sticky to contest,
        - its lens-relative nature is likely to disappear in reuse,
        - the blast radius if wrong is large and not containable,
        - it would gravitationally distort later interpretation,
        - revocation or supersession is unclear,
        - canon-ineligible support surfaces are being smuggled upward.

    A candidate may still reference canon-ineligible surfaces as supporting evidence, provided:

        - those surfaces are explicitly excluded from the promoted claim,
        - they are named in `canon_ineligible_attachments`,
        - and no later review posture treats them as canon by attachment.

### 7.5 Governability outputs

Recommended review outcomes at this gate:

- `governable_enough`
- `request_governability_clarification`
- `request_stronger_scope_fencing`
- `request_contestability_path`
- `request_revocation_rule`
- `reject_for_governability`

## 8. Canon eligibility rule

A candidate may promote only when all three of the following are true:

- legitimacy passes,
- utility passes,
- governability passes.

The safest early posture is:

- **narrow claim**
- **narrow use**
- **narrow scope**
- **low blast radius**
- **clear contestability**

This means early canon should be biased toward:

- baseline comparison anchors,
- recurring reference claims,
- narrowly scoped exclusion claims,
- or other stable low-radius reference surfaces.

Early canon should be biased against:

- rich semantic regime claims,
- global family claims,
- basin-as-ontology claims,
- support descriptors promoted as standalone truth,
- visually attractive summaries masquerading as canon.

---

## 9. Lens-scope rule

Early canon may be:

- single-lens only,
- lens-relative,
- or cross-lens stable.

But the scope must be explicit.

### 9.1 Single-lens canon

Allowed when:
- the claim is strong under one declared lens,
- the scope is narrow,
- and reuse does not require cross-lens generality.

### 9.2 Cross-lens canon

Stronger and preferred when:
- multiple declared lenses preserve the same bounded claim,
- without major ambiguity increase.

### 9.3 Lens-scope failure

A candidate should fail or be narrowed if:
- lens drift changes the claim materially,
- one lens supports the claim while another breaks it,
- or the reuse scope would silently invite universalization.

---

## 10. Ambiguity rule for canon

Not all ambiguity disqualifies canon.

The distinction is:

### 10.1 Harmless ambiguity
Ambiguity that does not change the proposed claim or its allowed use.

This may still permit promotion.

### 10.2 Authority-breaking ambiguity
Ambiguity that changes:
- the promoted claim,
- the safe scope,
- the allowed downstream use,
- or the likely blast radius if wrong.

This blocks promotion.

The packet must explicitly classify remaining ambiguity as:

- harmless,
- bounded,
- unresolved,
- or authority-breaking.

---

## 11. Consensus role

Consensus is not a generic scoring pass.

Consensus should inspect:

- claim boundedness,
- evidence sufficiency,
- ambiguity acceptability,
- scope discipline,
- reuse justification,
- governability,
- and allowed use boundaries. 

Consensus must not:

- treat packet existence as promotion,
- treat recurrence as truth,
- promote on stability alone,
- promote because review packaging is persuasive,
- overread substrate organization into ontology.

---

## 12. Canon-ineligible by default

The following are canon-ineligible by default unless later law explicitly narrows an exception:

- raw runtime summaries,
- unstable digest language,
- HUD or display packaging,
- convenience abstractions,
- unresolved probe-local heuristics,
- support descriptors as standalone truth objects,
- family-specific heuristics pretending universality,
- substrate organization taken directly as ontology,
- query results,
- dossier existence,
- readiness posture,
- preservation duration. 

These may support a canon claim.
They should not promote directly.

---

## 13. Contestability and supersession rule

Every promoted canon claim must remain contestable.

A lawful canon surface must preserve:

- a path to challenge,

At minimum, the contestability path should name:

    - one type of future evidence that would challenge the claim,
    - and one condition under which challenge review would be triggered.

- a path to narrow scope,
- a path to supersede,
- and a path to revoke if later evidence breaks the claim.

Canon must not become sticky merely because it was once lawful to promote.

Early canon should therefore be:

- reversible,
- reviewable,
- and low-blast-radius.

---

## 14. Minimal first canon rule

The smallest lawful first canon should probably be:

- a **narrow claim**
- attached to a well-supported packet
- under a declared lens
- with strong replay-honest lineage
- low authority-breaking ambiguity
- low blast radius if wrong
- and clear reuse compression benefit

The preferred first style is likely:

**trusted baseline comparison anchor**
or
**trusted recurring reference claim**

not:
- semantic regime object,
- basin ontology claim,
- transition mythology,
- or universal family law.

---

## 15. Decision surface

For any review, the final recommendation should be one of:

- `promote_candidate`
- `defer_candidate`
- `reject_candidate`
- `request_narrower_claim`
- `request_more_evidence`
- `request_lower_scope`
- `request_governability_clarification`

A candidate should promote only when the review can state, in plain language:

- what is trusted,
- under what conditions,
- for what use,
- with what non-claims,
- and how it may later be challenged.

---

## 16. Trial packet rule before activation

Before activating Door Two canon operationally, create at least three trial packets:

1. one likely-pass candidate
2. one likely-fail candidate
3. one ambiguous edge case

The ambiguous case is required.

Note that ambiguity may arise in at least two different ways:

- **evidence-edge** — support remains too weak, incomplete, or equivocal to stabilize the claim,
- **governability-edge** — support may be strong enough to review, but the claim cannot yet remain safely bounded as canon.

These should not be treated as the same type of edge case.
They usually require different review actions:
- evidence-edge → `request_more_evidence`
- governability-edge → `request_narrower_claim` or `request_governability_clarification`

Why:
pass/fail alone will not reveal where:
- ambiguity logic is still mushy,
- scope discipline is underdefined,
- or governability breaks under real review pressure.

These packets should be used as a Door One clarity audit and a Door Two legitimacy audit at the same time.

---

## 17. Working summary

Door Two canon activation should not be threshold-first, object-first, or poetry-first.

It should be:

- packet-first,
- claim-bounded,
- lens-declared,
- ambiguity-audited,
- utility-tested,
- and governability-checked.

A candidate should promote only when it is:

- legitimate enough to trust,
- useful enough to compress repeated lawful reuse,
- and governable enough to remain bounded, contestable, and non-distorting as a standing reference surface.
